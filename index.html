Initiate Pre-Accident Plan When
  • ✈️ Aircraft / Army Motor Vehicle accident
  • 🛎 Precautionary Landing (off-installation)
  • 🔧 Any damage to an aircraft
  • 🚩 Overdue Aircraft / Fallen Angel
  • 🟥 Death or critical injury to unit personnel
  • ⚠ Hazardous material spill
  • 📋 Any reportable incident in the CCIR
Accident Do's
✅Allow medical personnel to treat and transport the injured.
✅Allow military police and civil authorities to assist with security.
✅Direct questions from civil authorities to the senior military person present.
✅Secure classified/sensitive documents and equipment if possible without endangering personnel or disturbing wreckage.
Accident Do NOT's
❌Abandon the telephone that received the initial call unless relieved by CDR, XO, or S-3.
❌Discuss the accident with anyone except key personnel in this plan.
❌Interfere with crash rescue or medical personnel. Lifesaving is the first priority.
❌Interfere with or refuse to cooperate with military police or local authorities.
❌Move the injured unless fire is present or other immediate danger exists.
❌Approach a burning aircraft or vehicle except to save a life.
❌Disturb the wreckage except to save a life or treat the injured.
❌Give phone numbers to anyone except emergency or key personnel.
Upon completing notification, BOC begins DA Form 1594 Staff Duty Log and keeps it updated with all personnel/times contacted. Minimize use of primary phone line.

Mishap Information Worksheet
Step 1: Obtain as much information as possible — do NOT delay reporting basic information.
Duties & Responsibilities

Tap a role to expand checklist. Check off tasks as completed.

Battle Captain (S-3)
Initial response & coordination
⌄
  • Record/document information using Mishap Information Worksheet. Do not delay reporting.
  • Ensure appropriate first responders are notified.
  • Initiate separate staff journal (DA Form 1594) — log all events from time of notification.
  • Ensure battle tracking; notify S-3 to formulate options ASAP.
  • Re-direct aircraft/ground vehicles to incident as necessary.
  • Coordinate Search and Rescue through USAREUR-AF if necessary.
  • Monitor requests from crash site for special/additional equipment.
  • Request EOD support through Base Ops if ordinance is onboard. AAHP: DSN 587-0770 / IAHP: DSN 587-0782.
  • Coordinate with other units for aviation support as necessary.
  • Notify 12th CAB Ops Center DSN: 314-587-0816 / CIV: 09641-705870817.
  • Provide support to Accident Investigation Team. Coordinate aircraft support for AIB.
  • Conduct After-Action Review at earliest convenience.
  • Conduct and evaluate adequacy tests of the Pre-Accident Plan.
Commander
COL Bock — Overall control
⌄
  • Responsible for overall control and coordination of accident response.
  • Control, direct, coordinate, and dispatch primary crash alarm personnel, aircraft, equipment, and convoys to the accident site.
  • Notify the 12th CAB Command Group if required.
  • At discretion, temporarily ground all aircraft in Brigade if necessary. Resume ops as fuel/environmental/mechanical factors are ruled out.
  • If fatality or injuries occur, execute actions IAW Casualty SOP.
  • Discuss Flight Evaluation Board procedures with Standardization Officer and Safety Officer.
XO
MAJ Grubbs — Plan execution
⌄
  • In absence of Commander, assume CDR duties.
  • Supervise activation and execution of this plan to completion.
  • Ensure Brigade Safety Officer has been notified.
  • If severe injuries or fatality: ensure no unauthorized calls to next-of-kin.
  • Notify HHC Commander and unit Motor Officer in event of AMV accident.
  • Assume duties as CAB POC for the Battalion involved.
  • Determine composition and notify Brigade Crisis Action Team (CAT): Flight Surgeon, S-3, BAMO/AMO, S-1, AMSO, S-4, PAO, Chaplain, S-2, S-6, Rear-Det CDR (tactical).
  • Direct actions of Brigade CAT.
  • Continually update Brigade Commander on mishap status.
  • Monitor all reports to higher; coordinate actions with subordinates.
  • Assist and coordinate with media and outside agencies.
  • Coordinate all post-mishap actions.
Safety Officer / NCO
CW4 Bowyer — Site & investigation
⌄
  • Ensure BDE CDR and DCO are notified.
  • Ensure notification of V Corps Safety Director (with CAB CDR approval).
  • For suspected Class A or B: proceed to crash site and assist BN safety personnel.
  • At site: Take charge of mishap site, assist in security.
  • Secure all personal effects, ALSE, logbooks, PPC, maps, and info for investigation.
  • Identify witnesses and obtain all witness statements.
  • Initiate accident investigation; assist accident board if designated.
  • Take photographs/video of accident site.
  • Diagram mishap site.
  • Initiate Aviation Accident Report, Ground Accident Reports, or Initial Notification.
  • Make assessment of aircraft/AMV accident classification after AMO/SMO estimate.
  • Keep CDR, CSM, XO, and S-3 updated.
  • Contact V Corps Safety Office for AIB orders. DSN: 314-538-8778 / Cell: 502-936-2403.
  • Be prepared to participate as board member; recommend nominees to BN CDR.
  • Ensure equipment removed by accident board is accounted for by Property Book action.
  • Obtain Release of Interest memos from both investigating boards upon completion.
  • Review this plan quarterly; ensure participating agencies test at least quarterly.
  • Review accident reports before forwarding to reviewing authority.
S-1
CPT Hafen — Personnel & casualty
⌄
  • Obtain DD Form 93/SGLI, 201 field files, and photos for all crewmembers involved. Ensure casualty reports are filed.
  • Coordinate personnel administration functions.
  • Assist CDR in preparing letter of condolence and/or sympathy.
  • Coordinate with PAO (314-587-0778) to prepare generic incident summary for V Corps PAO press release.
  • Inform G-1/AG Casualty Section upon confirmation of casualties. Complete lines 31–43 of Casualty Report (AR 600-8-1, Ch. 3).
  • Only after final G-1/AG coordination: implement Death/Injury Notification Plan. Contact Casualty Section one final time.
  • Admin actions (promotions, awards, applicable actions).
S-2
CPT Grant — Intel & security
⌄
  • Check location for nearest threat; assess weather in area.
  • Coordinate with AMSO to submit SARIR with ISOPREP and EPA data for each survivor and survival equipment type.
  • Determine presence of classified documents/material/equipment on mishap aircraft and arrange security/recovery.
  • Coordinate with Military Police and local authorities if assistance needed.
  • Coordinate courier orders when required.
  • Provide terrain analysis (site, route to site, weather effects).
  • Provide imagery (GEOINT).
  • Provide 1:50,000 scale maps with best route to accident site.

BDE Flight Surgeon
MAJ Jones / MAJ Canipe — Medical
⌄
  • Be prepared to proceed to crash site, CSH/Hospital, or staging area per CDR guidance.
  • Ensure all FS assigned to 12th CAB know to conduct or be present for toxicology specimen draw if unit member involved.
  • Determine number, positions, and identities of personnel/casualties.
  • Crash Site: Secure Accident Investigation Handbook, Aircraft Mishap Kit, and trauma rucksack.
  • Ensure safety of casualties; appropriate treatment, stabilization, and evacuation. Assign triage classifications if necessary.
  • Ensure accident site disturbed as little as possible. Mark final resting position of each casualty. Act as site release authority for body removal.
  • Emergency Room: Ensure toxicology specimen collection from surviving crew. Coordinate with AFIP, US Army Aeromedical Research Lab, USACR/SC, and Pathology.
  • Conduct post-mishap physical examinations of surviving crew; issue DA Form 2992.
  • If fatalities: contact pathologist on call for autopsy coordination and site visit.
  • Secure medical and dental records.
  • Secure flight equipment of survivors and deceased. Do NOT remove helmet/flight equipment prior to autopsy.
  • Attend autopsy; ensure appropriate specimen collection.
  • Coordinate data handoff to flight surgeon on Aviation Accident Investigation Board.
  • Non-Injury Mishaps: Toxicology specimen collection from surviving crew. Coordinate with local FS/medical facility for access and results.
ASB Commander
DART Team assembly & dispatch
⌄
  • Coordinate with owning company to assemble DART team: aviation maintenance officer, ASO, MDS-specific TI, mechanic, and any specialty needed. At least one combat lifesaver.
  • Dispatch DART team to accident site ASAP.
  • Brief DART team: consolidate at assembly point before departing for site.
  • DART team assesses damage; provides Safety Officer initial ECOD for accident reporting.
  • DART team obtains fuel and oil samples; submits for analysis. Provide results to AIB.
  • DART team provides initial accident site security until owning unit arrives. Additional support through BN/BDE S3.

Tab D — PL & Overdue Aircraft
A Precautionary Landing (PL) or overdue aircraft may indicate a Fallen Angel. Initiate immediately.
Precautionary Landing Procedures
1
Attempt radio contact with aircraft on all available frequencies (tactical, guard 121.5 MHz, UHF/VHF).
2
Contact Tower/Base Ops for all airports/airfields along route of flight. AAHP: 314-587-0770 | IAHP: 314-587-6503.
3
Initiate Primary Crash Alarm. Notify Battle CPT (314-587-0774).
4
Notify CDR and key personnel per Secondary Alarm sequence.
5
Determine last known position (LKP), time, altitude, fuel state, and heading from flight plan and crew brief info.
6
Coordinate Search and Rescue (SAR) operations through USAREUR-AF if needed.
7
Be prepared to dispatch SAR aircraft. Ensure crew has survival equipment, maps, and comms.
8
S-2 conducts terrain analysis and provides best route to LKP/probable crash site.
9
Log all actions on DA Form 1594 from time aircraft declared overdue.
Fallen Angel Declaration
An aircraft is considered overdue when it fails to arrive within 30 minutes of ETA without contact.

Declare FALLEN ANGEL and initiate full pre-mishap plan when aircraft is confirmed missing or crashed.
Tab E — Accident Master Checklist

BOC/Battle Captain immediate actions upon notification.

Immediate Actions
  • Record time of notification in DA Form 1594.
  • Complete Mishap Information Worksheet (as much as possible).
  • Call emergency services if not already done (Fire/Rescue: 112, Law Enforcement: 110).
  • Call Primary Crash Alarm: Battle CPT/NCO DSN 314-587-0774.
  • Initiate Secondary Alarm notification sequence (see Contacts page).
  • Do NOT abandon primary phone. Minimize use to keep line open.
  • Do NOT discuss accident with anyone not on notification list.
Follow-On Actions
  • Ensure BOC tracking map/board updated with aircraft/incident location.
  • Request EOD if ordnance onboard (AAHP Base Ops: 314-587-0770).
  • Coordinate SAR aircraft as needed through S-3.
  • Ensure DART team dispatched from ASB.
  • Ensure Flight Surgeon / PA en route to crash site or hospital.
  • Ensure S-2 conducting threat assessment and terrain analysis.
  • Ensure S-1 obtaining personnel records for all crew/pax involved.
  • Ensure S-6 standing up communications support for AIB.
  • Monitor all reports; update DA Form 1594 continuously.
Items to Collect for Safety Investigation Team
  • Aircraft logbook and historical records
  • DA Form 2408-12 (Aircraft Status Log)
  • DA Form 2408-13 (Aircraft Inspection and Maintenance Record)
  • Crew flight records / Individual Flight Records
  • Aircrew ALSE (Aviation Life Support Equipment)
  • Helmets and personal flight equipment (do NOT remove before autopsy if fatality)
  • Maps and flight planning materials used for the mission
  • Applicable SOPs, ATM, FMs referenced for maneuvers being conducted
  • Weather data for time/location of mishap
  • ATC recordings and transcripts if available
  • Witness statements (all witnesses)
  • Photographs/video of accident site
  • Fuel and oil samples from surviving components
  • ISOPREP data for all crew
  • Medical and dental records
  • Toxicology specimens from surviving crew
  • Mission brief/flight brief documentation
  • Any classified documents/equipment from mishap aircraft
